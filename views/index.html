Ryan Johnson: Web Developer, JS enthusiast, fan of initials

There's nothing more exciting than a blank piece of paper, or a newly opened terminal console




I have always been a builder, turning lego blocks into entire worlds, walnut slabs into cabinets, and text chunks into beautiful prose. Now I build with blocks of code, creating magnificent web applications that improve people's lives and bring them joy.


This journey began as many do, with a hunger to create. I have worn many professional hats in the past--mentor, educator, researcher, entrepreneur, philanthropist--and have relished each of these roles. Through each of these, I am driven by the connection I have to others. If I can work to improve someone's life, job, or journey, I will do so with resolute vigor.


Yet it is in coding that I ultimately found fulfillment. Synergy formed between my desire to create and my ability to analyze and debug problems meticulously. I enrolled in an immersive web development program at General Assembly where I developed into a tenacious programmer with a keen eye for writing efficient and elegant JavaScript and zeal for solving problems. The things I create on a daily basis are both thrilling and functional.
